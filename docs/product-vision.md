# Product Vision

## Working Idea

This app is a memory system for Muay Thai training.

It helps fighters capture, clean up, organize, connect, and reuse what they learn in training before those details disappear.

The product should feel less like a textbook technique database and more like a practical training memory system: fast to capture, easy to browse, and useful when deciding what to practice next.

## Problem

Muay Thai students often learn useful drills, counters, coach cues, and sparring lessons, but do not record them anywhere. Even when they do write things down, notes tend to become scattered, hard to search, and disconnected from future training.

The result is that valuable training knowledge fades before it becomes usable.

## Core Insight

Combat sports knowledge is connected.

A single drill can involve pad work, boxing, head movement, footwork, counters, stance switching, and a specific uppercut sequence. The app should preserve those connections so the user can see what they have been working on and build future training from it.

## Product Promise

Capture what you learned. Let AI clean and categorize it. See how it connects. Turn it into something you can practice again.

## What This Is Not

- Not a generic notes app.
- Not a full Obsidian clone.
- Not a textbook martial arts encyclopedia.
- Not a fitness tracker in the first version.
- Not a workout app in the first version.

## Product Feel

The app should feel technical, organized, and alive. A slightly laboratory-like interface could fit, especially for the graph view, but the product should remain quick and practical after training.

The main feeling should be: "I can catch this before I forget it."
